TITANIC ANNIVERSARY IS 15th OF APRIL

On the 15th of April, we remember the tragic anniversary of the sinking of the RMS Titanic. This iconic ship, deemed "unsinkable," met its untimely demise on that fateful night in 1912.

The Titanic, a marvel of engineering and luxury, set sail on its maiden voyage from Southampton to New York City. On board were over 2,200 passengers and crew members, including some of the wealthiest individuals of the time.

However, disaster struck when the ship collided with an iceberg in the North Atlantic Ocean. Despite efforts to save the ship, the Titanic ultimately succumbed to the icy waters, resulting in the loss of over 1,500 lives.

The sinking of the Titanic remains one of the deadliest maritime disasters in history, serving as a stark reminder of the hubris of man and the unpredictable forces of nature. The tragedy sparked significant changes in maritime safety regulations and continues to captivate the world with its stories of heroism, sacrifice, and loss.

As we commemorate the anniversary of the Titanic, let us remember the lives lost and the lessons learned from this unforgettable event in history.
